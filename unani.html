Unani

What is Unani Medicine?

The Unani system of medicine is one of the oldest traditional healing systems, originating from ancient Greece and developed further by Arab and Persian scholars. It is based on the concept of balance in bodily humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile) and uses natural remedies to restore health.

Unani medicine integrates principles of holistic health, emphasizing the prevention of diseases and maintenance of health through diet, lifestyle, herbal remedies, and alternative therapies.

Principles of Unani Medicine

  • Four Humors: Blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile – balance is key to health.
  • Mizaj (Temperament): Every individual has a specific temperament that influences their health.
  • Tibb: Personalized medicine based on individual constitution and environment.
  • Ilaj: Modes of treatment include dietotherapy, pharmacotherapy, surgery, and regimental therapy.

Benefits of Unani Medicine

  • Effective for chronic ailments like asthma, arthritis, migraines, and skin conditions
  • Emphasizes prevention and lifestyle modification
  • Uses natural herbal and mineral preparations
  • Low risk of side effects when administered properly

Common Unani Treatments

  • Hijama (Cupping Therapy): Removes toxins and improves blood flow
  • Massage and Exercise: Promotes relaxation and strengthens the body
  • Herbal Formulations: Made from ingredients like saffron, amla, aloe vera, and honey
  • Dietary Advice: Personalized food plans based on temperament and condition

Unani in India

Unani medicine is formally recognized by the Government of India and promoted by the Ministry of AYUSH. Institutions like the Central Council for Research in Unani Medicine (CCRUM) conduct research, training, and public awareness.

Today, Unani treatments are offered in licensed hospitals and wellness centers across India and parts of South Asia, the Middle East, and Africa.
