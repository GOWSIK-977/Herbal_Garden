Homeopathy

What is Homeopathy?

Homeopathy is a holistic system of alternative medicine developed by Dr. Samuel Hahnemann in the late 18th century. It is based on the principle of "like cures like" — meaning a substance that causes symptoms in a healthy person can be used in small doses to treat similar symptoms in a sick person.

Homeopathic remedies are prepared through repeated dilution and shaking (potentization), and are prescribed based on the individual's physical, mental, and emotional symptoms. The treatment aims to stimulate the body's natural healing response.

Key Features

- Safe, gentle, and non-toxic
- Suitable for all age groups, including infants and pregnant women
- No side effects or drug interactions
- Individualized treatment plans

Common Remedies

Some widely used homeopathic remedies include:
- Arnica: for bruises and physical trauma
- Belladonna: for high fever with sudden onset
- Nux Vomica: for digestive disorders and hangovers
- Aconite: for shock or sudden illness

Homeopathy in India

Homeopathy is recognized and promoted by the Ministry of AYUSH in India. The Central Council for Research in Homoeopathy (CCRH) leads scientific studies, and numerous homeopathic colleges and hospitals serve the public across the country.
